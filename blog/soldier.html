About Soldiers

Indian soldiers are known for their bravery and dedication, with the Indian Army being the world's largest volunteer force and third-largest army. They serve in various capacities, from defending the country's borders to participating in international peacekeeping missions and providing humanitarian aid during natural disasters. The soldiers' motto is "Service Before Self," and their commitment to protecting the nation is honored annually on January 15th, Indian Army Day.

Core roles and characteristics

  • Defense and security: They are responsible for the nation's security, safeguarding its borders and interests.
  • Humanitarian aid: They participate in disaster relief operations, such as during floods and earthquakes, saving lives and providing assistance to affected communities.
  • International missions: The Indian Army contributes to global peace through its participation in UN peacekeeping missions.
  • Motto: Their guiding principle is "Service Before Self," which underscores the importance of placing the country's needs above personal interests.

Size and structure

  • Size: It is the world's second-largest military force, with over 1.4 million active personnel.
  • Structure: The army is an all-volunteer force and is organized into seven commands, with the basic field formation being the division.
  • Leadership: The army is headed by the Chief of the Army Staff, who holds the rank of General.

Specializations and history

  • High-altitude warfare: Indian soldiers are considered world-class in high-altitude and mountain warfare, with the High Altitude Warfare School being a sought-after training center.
  • Cavalry: It is one of the few militaries in the world to maintain a horse cavalry regiment, the 61st Cavalry, which is used for ceremonial purposes.
  • World Wars: During World War II, Indian soldiers fought on three continents, with their bravery recognized by many and their contribution deemed crucial by figures like Winston Churchill.

Recognition and remembrance

  • Army Day: Indian Army Day is celebrated on January 15th to honor the sacrifices and achievements of the soldiers. The date marks the day in 1948 when Lieutenant General K.M. Cariappa became the first Indian Commander-in-Chief.
  • Infantry Day: This is celebrated on October 27th to commemorate the landing of infantry troops in Srinagar in 1947, a critical turning point in the war.

Military Operations Names

The Indian Army has conducted numerous operations, including the annexations of Goa (Operation Vijay) and Hyderabad (Operation Polo), counter-insurgency missions like Operation Blue Star and Operation Woodrose, and major campaigns like Operation Meghdoot in the Siachen Glacier. The army also participates in evacuation and humanitarian assistance, as seen in recent operations such as Operation Ganga (from Ukraine) and Operation Kaveri (from Sudan)

Notable military operations

  • Operation Vijay: Annexation of Goa, Daman and Diu from Portuguese rule in 1961.
  • Operation Polo: of Hyderabad into the Indian Union in 1948.
  • Operation Meghdoot: Capture of the majority of the Siachen Glacier in 1984.
  • Operation Blue Star: A 1984 military operation to remove militants from the Golden Temple in Amritsar.
  • Operation Cactus: Intervention in the Maldives in 1988 to prevent a coup.
  • Operation Pawan: The Indian Peace Keeping Force's (IPKF) mission in Sri Lanka beginning in 1987.

Humanitarian and evacuation operations

  • Operation Ganga: Evacuation of Indian citizens from Ukraine in 2022.
  • Operation Kaveri: Evacuation of Indian citizens from Sudan in 2023.
  • Operation Ajay: Evacuation of Indian nationals from Israel and Palestine in 2023.
  • Operation Raahat: Evacuation of citizens from Yemen in 2015.
  • Operation Sukoon: Evacuation of citizens from Lebanon during the 2006 war.
